How to Play

Three Men's Morris is a slightly more complex cousin of Tic-Tac-Toe - but you still win by getting three in a row. The game is played in two phases:


1.

Players get three pieces each. First, you have to place your pieces on the board - blocking your opponent from getting three in a row, while setting yourself up for a win.


2.

With all of the pieces on the board, you'll take turns moving them around. But there's a catch: you can't move a piece into an adjacent spot. The game is played until someone gets three of their pieces in a row - horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.
